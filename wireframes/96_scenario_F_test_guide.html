What you're testing

Scenario F is the IO overseeing the National Committee network. The worked example is UWC Kenya: a volunteer roster with colour-coded training compliance, MoU obligations (one overdue 58 days), the NC Chair's scoped portal view, and the network-wide IO NC Health Dashboard. Two nightly functions keep it honest: one marks MoUs overdue, one recomputes volunteer training status.

Three things prove the scenario: the training colour chips (CURRENT/EXPIRED/MISSING), the MoU overdue state, and the role-scoping (Grace sees only Kenya).

Training_Status is verified live; other F field names are not

NC_Volunteers.Training_Status is confirmed live as a colour-coded picklist verified (CURRENT/EXPIRING SOON/EXPIRED/MISSING). Other F module fields are tagged confirm — verify before relying on them. The IO NC Health Dashboard has its own build guide.

Manual build — not API-creatable

WR-F-1/F-2, the four scheduled functions, list-view conditional formatting (NC colour banding by MoU_Health), and the NC Chair portal profile must be built in Setup. The REST API confirms fields, not these.

How the two nightly jobs feed the views
MoU Due_Date < today & not done ──▶ Obligation_Status = Overdue (mark_mou_overdue) ──▶ count overdue per NC ──▶ MoU_Health = Critical/Attention (recompute_mou_health) ──▶ NC list banding + IO dashboard KPI Volunteer training records ──▶ CURRENT / EXPIRED / MISSING (recompute_volunteer_training_status) ──▶ roster chips + "Training Gaps" KPI (EXPIRED or MISSING)

1. Confirm UWC Kenya seed: 3 volunteers — Grace (CURRENT), Daniel (EXPIRED), Aisha (MISSING); 4 MoU obligations incl. Annual Report Q2 2026 overdue ~58 days.
2. Pre-seed MoU_Health = Critical on Kenya — recompute_mou_health runs only overnight.
3. Decide the NC Chair view: Option A (real user "Grace Omondi" with NC Chair Portal profile) or Option B (Switch User). Roles must be configured before profile restrictions take effect.
