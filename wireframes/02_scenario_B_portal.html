Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about UWC, selection, and the application process

What is UWC and how is it different from other schools?

UWC (United World Colleges) is a global movement of 18 schools and colleges across four continents, plus 130+ National Committees in 150+ countries. UWC offers the International Baccalaureate Diploma combined with experiential learning rooted in service, outdoor education and creativity.

Students are selected to reflect the diversity of the world — typically 80+ nationalities per campus — and live together in residence. The mission is to make education a force to unite people, nations and cultures for peace and a sustainable future.

How much does UWC cost? Are scholarships available?

Most students receive financial assistance. Scholarships are funded by individual donors, foundations, corporations and government partners, and are awarded by the National Committee in your country of residence.

The Brazil National Committee considers financial need as part of the selection process — please complete the financial information section of your application honestly. Selection is not affected by need; the level of scholarship awarded is determined separately, after a place has been offered.

How are students selected?

Each National Committee runs its own selection process, which typically includes a written application, interviews, and group activities. The process is committee-led and based on potential, motivation, and fit with UWC values — not solely academic grades.

Brazil’s 2026/27 process has three rounds: Eligibility Review, Local Selection (interview + group activities in São Paulo), and National Selection (final panel).

What happens after I submit my application?

Your application enters Eligibility Review (1–2 weeks). If eligible, you’ll be invited to a Local Selection round with interviews and group activities (June). National Selection happens in July. Place allocation — the specific UWC school you’ll attend — is confirmed by mid-August.

You can track every stage in the Application status tab of this portal.

Can I choose which UWC school I attend?

You can indicate preferences in your application, but final school assignment is determined by the National Committee and UWC International based on fit and availability. Most NCs prioritise fit (campus programme, location, community match) over preference.

Each of the 18 UWC schools has its own character — some are larger, some focus more on outdoor education, some are urban. We encourage applicants to be open to any campus.

What if my circumstances change during the application process?

Update your portal immediately and notify your National Committee at brazil@uwc.org. Significant changes — e.g. moving country, a change in family financial situation, a health update — may affect your application.

Contact us early. We can usually accommodate changes that are flagged in good time; we cannot fix things we don’t know about.

What documents do I need to upload?

For Brazil applicants in the 2026/27 cycle, you need:

1. Passport scan (photo page only). 2. School transcript covering the last 2 academic years. 3. Recommendation letter from a teacher who knows you well. 4. Guardian consent (signed by your parent or legal guardian if you’re under 18).

The Documents tab in this portal lists exactly what’s needed and accepts PDF, JPG or PNG up to 10 MB each.

Who can I contact if I have problems with my application?

Email your National Committee directly at brazil@uwc.org, or use the Messages tab to send a question that will be routed to your reviewer.

For technical portal issues (login problems, upload failures, missing data), email support@uwc.org with your reference number (UWC-2026-BR-00127) in the subject line.
