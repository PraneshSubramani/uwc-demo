Opening — set the scene (5 min)

Land the frame before any clicks: this is one platform that carries an applicant from the public portal all the way to a confirmed school place, while quietly enforcing the rules UWC cares about — eligibility, parental consent, blind review, safeguarding, data protection, and GDPR.

"Over the next hour you'll meet Sofia, a sixteen-year-old from São Paulo, and follow her real journey through our platform. Along the way you'll meet the people who support her — her National Committee, two independent reviewers, the International Office, and a partner college. Everything you see is live in a Zoho CRM environment configured for UWC, on UK and EU data centres. Let's begin where Sofia's committee begins: setting up."

CSelection, blind scoring & safeguardingClara & Paulo · Reviewers
The integrity heart of the demo: two reviewers score independently and cannot influence each other, and a welfare concern instantly protects an applicant's record.
1Clara · ReviewerFirst reviewer scores Sofia
DoAs Clara, score Sofia on five criteria (Academic Potential, Community Engagement, Communication, UWC Values — 1–10 — plus an overall recommendation). Submit.
2Paulo · ReviewerSecond reviewer sees nothing of the first
DoSwitch to Paulo (incognito). Open Sofia — Clara's scores are hidden. Paulo scores independently and submits.
Say"This is the most important safeguard in selection. Until both reviewers commit, neither can see the other — no anchoring, no influence."
3Ana · NC AdminBoth scores unlock — together
DoAs the committee, open Sofia — now both score sets are visible side by side.
SeeThe moment the second review landed, the platform unlocked both.
4Ana · NC AdminA welfare concern protects an applicant
DoOpen Miguel Santos — a safeguarding flag is already raised. His record is locked from progression with a red banner.
Say"The instant a welfare concern is raised, the application freezes and the right people are alerted. Nobody can quietly progress or alter it while it's investigated."
5Ana · NC AdminPanel decision for Sofia
DoRecord the panel decision: Shortlisted — "Strong candidate, clear alignment with UWC values."
SeeBlueprint moves Under Review → Shortlisted and Sofia gets a congratulations email.
Workflow: each submitted review increments a count; at two, the platform flips "all reviews complete" and unlocks both score sets. The Shortlisted gate requires ≥2 assessments, no open safeguarding flags, and a recorded panel decision.
Safeguarding: raising a flag locks the record from all stage progression and emails the NC Admin + International Office immediately. (Miguel's was pre-created — shown, not raised live.)
Land this with the panel
  • Blind review is enforced by the platform — fairness isn't left to good behaviour.
  • A safeguarding concern produces an instant, automatic lockdown — child protection first.
  • An application can't be shortlisted while a safeguarding flag is open — the gate won't allow it.
How does the blind review actually work?
Each reviewer's scores are visible only to them until a count of two submissions trips the unlock — explained honestly as a field-visibility rule driven by the Blueprint.
