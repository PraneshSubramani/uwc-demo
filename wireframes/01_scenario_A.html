Personal Statement multi-line
Growing up near Cologne, I witnessed the 2021 flood devastation first-hand. The Ahr valley — a region my family had visited every summer — was unrecognisable overnight. This experience ignited my passion for environmental justice and cross-border cooperation. Through my work with the local climate youth group, I helped organise three community forums that brought together farmers, scientists, and local government. I learned that sustainable change requires not just technical solutions but genuine dialogue across social boundaries. At UWC, I hope to connect with peers from climate-vulnerable regions in the Global South — from Bangladesh to Mozambique — and build collaborative solutions that transcend national boundaries. I believe education is the most powerful lever for the systemic change our generation urgently needs.
Education Background multi-line
Gymnasium Köln-Porz · Year 11 (2025–2026) Current GPA: 1.3 (German scale) — equivalent A*/A across all subjects Strong in Biology, Chemistry, Geography, and English Literature. Abitur expected June 2027. Additional: DELF B2 (French), Cambridge Advanced English (C1 distinction)
Extracurricular Activities multi-line
• Youth Climate Forum Cologne — co-founder (2023–present). Organised 3 public forums, 400+ attendees total. • School debate team — regional finalist 2024 (topic: EU climate policy) • Volunteer: Ahr valley flood relief, summer 2021 and 2022 • Violin — Grade 8 distinction, performs in school chamber orchestra • Football — school team captain, 2024–2025 season
Leadership Statement multi-line
My approach to leadership is rooted in listening before acting. When I co-founded the Youth Climate Forum, my first instinct was to survey 200 classmates about what environmental topics they actually cared about — not what I assumed they would. That survey led us to focus on local flooding rather than abstract global targets, which tripled our attendance at the first event. I have learned that effective leadership means making complexity accessible, and that the most important skill is creating space for others to contribute their knowledge.
NC Local Question Answer multi-line
"Die Klimakrise betrifft meinen Wohnort direkt — steigende Hochwasser im Rheintal. Ich würde lokale Klimaschutzinitiativen verbinden und junge Menschen zur Mitwirkung motivieren, indem ich einen interdisziplinären Ansatz verfolge: Naturwissenschaft, Politik und soziale Gerechtigkeit als ein gemeinsames Projekt."
