Personal Statement
Growing up in São Paulo, I learned early that opportunity is not evenly shared. I spent two years tutoring younger students in my community's library on weekends — what began as homework help became a small reading club of fourteen children. Seeing them grow in confidence taught me that education is the most durable form of hope. I am drawn to UWC because I want to learn alongside people whose lives have been shaped by circumstances different from my own. I believe the deepest learning happens when difference is not smoothed over but understood. At Atlantic, I hope to study marine science and continue the community work that has defined my life so far — carrying it into a context far larger than my neighbourhood.
